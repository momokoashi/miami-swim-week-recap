02
Section two

GOOD — could be better ⚠️

Right ideas, weak execution. Keep them — fix how we run them.

Could be better · the concept

👵 “Grandma owns one”

  • Strong idea — but very weak on site, basically nothing happening
  • Banner was hard to read; booth setup had to changeNo one really saw the lemon inflatable or the concept. Mockup ≠ reality on the day.
  • Reaction was just “oh, okay” — not what we wanted
Fix Grandma must be loud + aggressive at all times. Run it as a “Nancy Circus” act — give it traffic, volume, and time.
Could be better · brand clarity

🍦 The popsicle's blind spot

  • The quote wasn't “out there” enough — many never read it
  • People remove the wrapper → Nancy disappears, no photoThe branding lived on the thing people throw away.
  • Many thought we were a popsicle brand, not a toy brand
Fix Put Nancy branding on the food itself (branded molds — e.g. plug-shaped popsicles). Make “what we actually do” impossible to miss.
Could be better · the assets

We left content on the table

  • Forgot the product launches. No snack pack; plushie & Every Mood under-featuredTreated as “nice to have” instead of the point.
  • Creator reach unused. They feared posting on their main accounts (worried views would tank)
Fix Deliberate shot list per launch · book separate shoot time with creators · offer a new/separate profile · lock deliverables for anyone paid or in-kind.
03
Section three

BAD — never again 🚫

Scrap these. They cost us and they don't come back.

Never again · the capture gap

⏰ Nobody posted in real time

  • Only started after Day 1 when Rahul asked “why aren't we posting?”Too late — we missed the live moment and could only post after the fact.
  • No reaction content captured — no live videos of people reacting at the booth
Fix Same-day pipeline: shoot → upload → organize → post the same day. Some posts straight from the phone. Run a livestream. Assign one person to live reactions.
Never again · outsourcing blind

🧨 We outsourced blind, with no pipeline

  • Cold viral editor failed. Didn't get the brand; footage was an unorganized pile of clips/scripts → confusing for anyone not on the shoot → he quit
  • No editing/posting pipeline set up beforehand. No standby editor; didn't line up Content ForgeSo no one posted until massively after the fact. Best post ~70k, everything else far lower — we grew followers but not virally.
Fix Never hand over raw chaos — brief + organize first, and line up the editor/pipeline before the event.
Never again · relying on the organizer

🏢 We relied on the organizer

  • They committed nothing. Miami Swim Week only said “maybe we'll share on socials” — no committed creator posting
  • Unresponsive on logistics. We didn't even know the booth was on the 2nd floor with very low visibilityThe organizer wasn't getting back to us, and we didn't chase it.
Fix Require committed posting · chase & confirm booth placement ahead of the day · since we're not yet great at virality, anchor influencer views & content ourselves — don't rely on a partner's “maybe.”
Never again · the money

💸 Open-ended spend, no framework

  • We're fine spending if CPM ≈ $1 — but we set no ceiling → overspent
  • “$1 CPM” isn't understood by the team — it only works if we maximize creatorsWe can't expect the team to intuit it. We have to engineer the creativity and incentives.
Fix Set a spend ceiling per event. Build and control the influencer + clipper engine — that's the lever that makes the CPM math real.

Playbook · run it tight

Process & content engine

🗂️ Process & planning

  • Start from goals → context → keep / fix / scrap
  • Aggressive checklist + project management
  • Invite 1–2 weeks out with RSVP-to-attend
  • Lock deliverables for any creator (paid or in-kind)

🎬 Content engine

  • Same-day shoot → upload → organize → post
  • Some posts straight from the phone
  • Run a livestream
  • Capture live reactions on purpose
  • Showcase what works (popsicles) — fix the branding-on-food gap
Playbook · the multipliers

Creators & the Nancy Circus

🌟 Creators / partnerships

  • Keep inviting creators (~$1k) — build community
  • Offer separate profiles to protect their views
  • Activate IM8, Dr. Tina — “what do we have?”
  • Partner with events whose creators actually post — e.g. Snowflake Gelato normally gets 3–6k, but bring food creators and posts hit 30–40k at events
  • Or partner with brands that keep influencers on retainer — they bring their roster

🎪 On-site spectacle

  • Paid actors on shift — dancing 4 hrs, making noise irrespective of the crowd (critical)
  • Colored sparkly water (pink/yellow glitter) for the lamps & toys
  • Branded food (plug-shaped popsicles)
  • Test “share a story → get the food” & measure who posts
Playbook · merch & ops

The free-advertising layer

👜 Big tote bags = walking ads (the Alo play) 🎁 Cost-analyze gifting volume 👕 Merch + order prep, T-shirts 📲 Make products buyable on-site (scan & pay) 🎥 Hire local videography per city (stay hands-on)

A bag people want to carry is free marketing from the booth to the taxi to the next party.
