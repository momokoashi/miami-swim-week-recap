A reflection — not a to-do list. Repeat what was great, fix what was good, kill what was bad. Built for every event after this one.

Why we went · the goal we never hit

The goal was content value — and we missed it.

< $1
Target CPM

Views needed to hit it
  • We never calculated, realistically, how many views we needed — and had no game plan
  • The result: zero viral videos
Two reasons ① the CPM goal may have been too aggressive · ② we overspent with no plan to get the views (incl. PR + influencers). Next time: start from the view target, then work backward.
01
Section one

GREAT — do it again ✅

The stuff that worked. Repeat it by default at every event.

Great · the magnet

🍦 The popsicle giveaway

  • Last-minute idea — it was so hot, free food, everyone came for it
  • The popsicle pulled crowds right in front of the product~$7 each from a local vendor. Pricey, but it worked.
  • Shy to post a sex toy → happy to post a popsicle
Next time Brand on the food itself — the wrapper sticker was too small and people toss it, so the Nancy branding never landed. Test branded drinks (lemonade) too.

Free food always works. 🙌

Great · the booth

🐠 The fish tank stopped traffic

  • The Lem squirting in the tank = amazing reaction + instant laugh
  • People understood what it does in secondsWithout it, passers-by don't get it and just walk past.
Next time Make it stop people from the walkway — they only came over when asked. Bigger tank? Colourful / glittery water as the hero visual?
Great · vibe & collaboration

🤝 The friend vibe was real

  • We hired friends → genuine collaboration, everyone shooting each other
  • Creators + models + their partners gave us couple / relationship contentBringing a friend beats hiring cold — and the relationship compounds.
Next time Set a clear content KPI — people shot but didn't post. Invite influencers who already know how to shoot + post viral. Use people like Susan fully (let her post without approval), ask creators to spin up a fresh profile so they post freely, and tighten the shotlist + deliverables.
Great · gifting

🎁 Gifting the product always works

  • On Day 3 we gave out the excess product → people came (“heard you're gifting”) and got way more engagedFree product is the most reliable crowd switch we have.
Next time Gift intentionally from the start, not just to offload leftover stock.

Spin-the-wheel got a few low-friction follows (TikTok + IG) — nice, but it didn't move anything.

02
Section two

GOOD — could be better ⚠️

Right ideas, weak execution. Keep them — fix how we run them.

Could be better · the concept

👵 “Grandma owns one”

  • Fun concept — but the execution didn't land. Grandma wasn't loud enough
  • Low booth traffic, so the “crazy grandma” never got captured
  • 2nd-floor booth: banner hard to read, inflatable in an awkward spot no one used — and the mockup was useless (no time to check on-site)
Next time Brief paid actors with a hard deliverable — always ON, in people's faces, dancing/singing weird (“Nancy Circus”). And confirm booth placement ahead so the set actually works.
Could be better · the reaction cam

🎥 Reaction camera = gold

  • Great idea — we captured priceless footagePeople's reactions to a sex toy are pure gold.
  • But we only ran it on Day 3 — most of the event went uncaptured
Next time Keep the reaction cam rolling all the time, every day, from open to close.
Could be better · the product shoot

📸 The product shoot wasn't thought through

  • We scheduled it — but didn't plan it around which products we're launching or which assets we're missing
  • On top of the fun content, this is where we make merch + launch assetsForgot the snack pack; plushie & Every Mood under-featured.
Next time Build the shot list around upcoming launches + merch — not just fun content. Know the missing assets before we shoot.
03
Section three

BAD — never again 🚫

Scrap these. They cost us and they don't come back.

Never again · the capture gap

⏰ Nothing got posted on site

  • Day 1: nothing went out. We took tons of shots but got stuck “organizing”
  • The 3 parts: ① shoot (good ✓) · ② organize + upload fast · ③ edit + postParts ② and ③ were missing — so we never captured the media value.
Never again Post on-site as we film. One person dedicated to grinding upload → edit → post in real time. Some posts straight from the phone.
Never again · the external editor

🧨 The external editor couldn't help

  • He didn't understand the footage or the brand
  • The clips/scripts were an unorganized pile → confusing for anyone not on the shoot → he quitBest post landed ~70k; everything else far lower — we grew followers, but not virally.
Never again Organize footage as it's shot, and only bring in support that actually understands the brand — brief them properly first.
Never again · no PR, no influencers

📣 We didn't drive PR or influencers

  • No PR push, and we didn't get influencers to come and post
  • Deliverables were unclear — and the product category makes people shy to postSo they came, had a nice time, and very little went out organically.
Never again Lock PR + influencers ahead with clear, written deliverables. Assume nothing posts organically — engineer it.
Never again · no budget or goal framework

💸 No budget & goal framework

  • We overspent (~$100k) with no budget framework and no spend ceiling
  • And no view target or game plan to hit the < $1 CPM“$1 CPM” only works if we engineer the views — the team can't intuit it.
Never again Set the spend ceiling AND the view target up front, then build backward. Control the influencer + clipper engine — that's the lever.
The takeaway

Repeat the great. Fix the good. Kill the bad.

And start every event from the view target — then work backward to the spend.

Run it like a content engine — and bring the Nancy Circus energy (everyone playing a role, making noise their own way) when the event calls for it. 🎪
